Adaptive Optics

Dynamic correction of optical aberrations within complex telescopes.

Mis-alignments

For a space telescope to be able to see at its best, all of its components must be in perfect alignemnt. This is a little difficult when the system is launched o n the back of a rocket to accelerate through the atmosphere! It also may be constantly heated and cooled if it orbits around the Earth, gradually moving elements within the telescope.

A solution

To attempt to correct for these issues to bring the system back into perfect alignment and acheive pin-sharp images, we use a technology known as "Active Optics". This involves sensing how the system has become mis-aligned, and fixing that - usually using a mirror that can compenstate by changing its shape.

Wavefront Sensing

Before any correction can be performed however, we must know what correction is required. This is what I have been working on - a technique known as "wavefront-sensing". Dedicated devices and complex mathematical formula can be used to build up a picture of how the light has been altered, and then send some commands to the deformable mirror to fix it.
