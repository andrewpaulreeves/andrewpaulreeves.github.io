Atmospheric Turbulence

Light from distant stars and galaxies passes perfectly through billions of miles of space. When it enters the Earths atmosphere though, that changes. Turbulent air adversly affect the light and causes optical "aberrations" in astronomical images. This results in blurring and loss of detail.

Next

Adaptive Optics

Adaptive Optics is a cutting edge technology which can correct for this atmospheric turbulence. Bright stars are used as reference sources to measure the turbulence. A deformable mirror, a thin reflective foil sheet backed by many actuators which change the mirrors shape, is updated thousands of times a second in order to keep astronomical images ultra-sharp.
